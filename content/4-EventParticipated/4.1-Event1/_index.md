# Summary Report: "AWS: Enterprise Cloud Architectures and Industry Application featuring Cloud Kinetics & Renova Cloud"

**Event Date:** July 4, 2026

### Event Overview

This event brought together industry professionals and students to discuss the evolving landscape of cloud computing, focusing on enterprise architectures, career development in cloud technologies, and practical applications in data engineering.

### Speakers

- **Representatives from Cloud Kinetics & Renova Cloud** – Industry practitioners sharing enterprise cloud experiences
- **Banh Cam Vinh** – Data Engineer with 5 years of experience across Siêu Việt Group, Heineken, and ZaloPay
- **Nhu Tran** – Account Manager at AWS
- **Various industry experts** – Discussing cloud-first strategies and professional development

### Key Highlights

#### Cloud Computing Trends and Market Overview

- **Cloud-first strategy** has become inevitable for major enterprises
- While entry costs may be low, the total cloud market scale has far exceeded traditional hardware infrastructure
- Enterprise adoption is accelerating, making cloud skills essential for technology professionals

#### Professional Skills and Career Development

**Networking and Career Opportunities:**
- 90-100% of job opportunities at major technology companies come through internal recruiting or referrals
- Building professional relationships (networking) is crucial for career advancement
- International certifications primarily help pass AI-powered resume screening but don't replace actual capabilities

**Professional Mindset:**
- Always ask "Why?" to develop critical thinking
- Learn from failures and mistakes
- Maintain a "stay hungry" attitude for continuous improvement
- Develop both technical and soft skills

#### Data Engineering in Practice

**Banh Cam Vinh's Journey (5 years experience):**
- Worked across diverse environments: Siêu Việt Group, Heineken, ZaloPay
- Emphasized that engineers must understand business operations, not just technical implementation
- Successful solutions require balancing technical capabilities with business requirements
- Cloud services should be orchestrated like assembling a football team – each service plays a specific role

#### Soft Skills and Professional Growth

**Key Insights from Nhu Tran (AWS Account Manager):**
- Overcoming fear of failure is essential for growth
- Don't be afraid of management or senior colleagues – proactive communication is valuable
- Initiative and clear communication separate good professionals from great ones

**Professional Development Advice:**
- Go beyond textbook knowledge
- Focus on real-world problem-solving thinking
- Learn to integrate multiple cloud services effectively
- Continuously network to create opportunities

### Key Takeaways

#### Technical Understanding
- Cloud computing is not just about technology – it's about business transformation
- Understanding how to combine different cloud services is more important than knowing individual services in isolation
- Data engineers must bridge the gap between technical capabilities and business needs

#### Career Development
- Certifications open doors but practical skills keep them open
- Networking is the primary path to opportunities in major tech companies
- Continuous learning and staying current with cloud trends is essential

#### Professional Approach
- Proactive communication and initiative are valued highly
- Learning from failure is part of professional growth
- Business understanding is as important as technical expertise
- "Stay hungry" – maintain curiosity and drive for improvement

### Applying to Work

**Immediate Applications:**
- Develop deeper understanding of business context for technical projects
- Focus on integrating multiple AWS services to solve real problems
- Build professional network through active participation in tech communities
- Practice asking "Why?" to understand root causes and requirements

**Long-term Development:**
- Pursue practical cloud projects that demonstrate integrated service knowledge
- Develop soft skills alongside technical capabilities
- Build relationships in the cloud computing community
- Focus on understanding business value, not just technical implementation
